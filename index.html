// 01 — ABOUT

Designing the
Future of Wireless

I am a final-year Electronics and Communication Engineering student at Sai Vidya Institute of Technology, Bengaluru — with a deep focus on antenna design, RF systems, and microwave engineering.

My simulation work spans HFSS-designed patch antennas achieving S11 of –32 dB, through to 4×4 phased arrays with active beam-steering at 3.5 GHz — all targeting real-world 5G deployment.

At DRDO LRDE, I work on defence-grade RF and radar systems. I also serve as IEEE Secretary and Chair of four society student branches at SVIT, driving technical growth across the department.

PROJECT / 01

High-Performance Sub-6 GHz Patch Antenna with Crescent DGS for 5G

HFSS-designed microstrip patch antenna at 3.5 GHz for 5G NR Band n78. A crescent-shaped defected ground structure dramatically boosted return loss and gain beyond a plain patch baseline.

S11: –32 dBGain: 4.06 dB 3.5 GHzHFSS

● PUBLISHED — IEEE Xplore / ICMNWC 2025

PROJECT / 02

4×4 Phased Array with Beam Steering for Sub-6 GHz 5G

Designed and simulated a 16-element phased array on FR4 substrate with full beam-steering capability. Demonstrates high gain and efficient radiation for 5G base station deployment at 3.5 GHz.

S11: –22.5 dBGain: 6.87 dB 4×4 ArrayBeam Steering

● ACCEPTED — WAMS 2026 Oral Presentation

RaNaT: IoT-Based Dual-Axis Solar Tracking System
